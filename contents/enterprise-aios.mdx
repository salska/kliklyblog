The AI strategy of many enterprises is still at the “let’s get a site license for Claude” stage of maturity if you are lucky.

Many are probably still blocking access to AI tools entirely as a “security measure” which feels increasingly pointless when every employee has a smartphone with unrestricted access to frontier models in their pocket.

> Some enterprises may be tokenising every SharePoint site, shared drive, Confluence page, Teams transcript, and PDF into a giant AI knowledge layer that can interface with selected applications.

That will absolutely create value. Better retrieval, onboarding, summarisation, support, and enterprise search are all real wins.

But this falls way short of the agentic OS opportunity and landscape that is forming. The enterprise instinct to apply the old rules to AI whilst useful, it is not transformative. Yes I used that word!

The real shift is when each and every organisation creates its dedicated and custom AI Operating System to streamline planning, reduce latency and automate workflows. 

> The future is probably not “employees using AI tools.” It is enterprises themselves becoming AI-shaped. 

Guess what, the people you are selling to now has the tools and capabilities to supercharge their intelligence and think much more rationally about what they want and why it makes a difference. So its imperative that enterprises dig a little deeper. Enterprise Grade AI Operating Systems.
